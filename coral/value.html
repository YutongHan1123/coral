Value of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are worth an estimated $172 billion per year.

Worldwide, coral reefs provide us with an array of benefits. Their ecosystem services include:

  • Marine Biodiversity
    •
    • Despite occupying less than 1% of marine habitat, reefs host 25% of all marine species at some point in their lives.
    • If you want to know more about coral reef biodiversity, click here.
  • Fisheries Production
    •
    • Reefs provide important nurseries and habitat for commercial and recreational fisheries, like those for mahi mahi and spiny lobster, among other fish, here in South Florida.
    • If you want to know more about the importance of reef fisheries, click here.
  • Recreation and Tourism
    •
    • Reefs create thousands of jobs in the tourism, diving, snorkeling and fishing industries.
  • Sand Production
    •
    • Corals and other reef organisms build skeletons by creating calcium carbonate from minerals in the water. Due to erosion from natural causes, these minerals break down and wash up onshore to create beaches over long periods of time.
  • Coastal Protection
    •
    • A healthy barrier reef can reduce an estimated 97% of wave energy on average (Ferrario et al. 2014). These benefits are even more important during storms and hurricanes that are so common in many areas of the tropical world that are also home to coral reefs.
    • If you want to know more about coastline protection from green infrastructure, click here.
