Laboratory Wave Tank Tests

The University of Miami Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science houses the world’s largest indoor hurricane simulator and wave tank, the Surge Structure Atmosphere Interaction facility (SUSTAIN).

Pilot Deployment of AR Models

While our measurements in the laboratory are useful to predict the types of benefits we might see in the real world, we also need to make sure that our hybrid reef breakwater designs are practical and effective in the real world.

Use of Climate-Resilient Corals

It’s not enough to simply restore corals back to an environment in which they are still threatened. In addition to many local threats, climate-driven ocean warming causes a process called thermal bleaching.

Urban Impact Surveys

Coastal protection solutions like our hybrid reef/breakwater create benefits for the people who live and work along the coast. To quantify and predict these benefits, our team’s architecture and urban planning experts are assessing the urban vulnerability of sections along Miami Beach’s shoreline.

Public Engagement

Our work is contingent upon the support and engagement of our communities. For this reason, we built a communications strategy to inform, engage, and win support from stakeholders in the City of Miami Beach.
