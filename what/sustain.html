Laboratory Wave Tank Tests

Laboratory Wave Tank Tests

To test the effect on incoming waves of the coral reef cluster alone (green infrastructure), the trapezoidal structure alone (grey infrastructure), and the combined hybrid reef breakwater (gray/green infrastructure), we carefully measured wave height and energy at points along a cross section of the tank, generally before, on top of, and after the barrier. The results show us how a wave changes as it passes over the barrier, and how much wave height and energy is reduced before it reaches the simulated shoreline.

The reduction of the wave height and energy due to the hybrid reef breakwater is compared with the reduction due to the coral alone and the structure alone. These measurements help us to better understand both the relative contribution of each of the three types of barriers to reducing wave energy and wave height and what potential benefits our real-world shorelines would experience if such reefs were deployed in ocean waters. The measurements also help understand the effects of wave conditions in the performance of such structures.
